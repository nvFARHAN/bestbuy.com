Visibility and recognition are a crucial part of our digital future. And digital equity is only possible if we recognize the full diversity of Asian American and Pacific Islander communities. That’s why we’re elevating stories and voices from a range of cultures and backgrounds.

Musician

Maia // mxmtoon

At 17 years old, Maia began posting covers of pop songs and her own teenage ditties under the screen name, mxmtoon. Since then, her passion for music along with the help of tech has turned her into a streaming sensation. In 2019, she released her full-length album, the masquerade, which featured RIAA Gold single "prom dress." She’s been highlighted in multiple publications, with Billboard magazine calling her “one of the sharpest minds in music today.” Her second album, rising, will be released in May.

Best Buy’s commitment to equity

We’ll spend $1.2 billion with BIPOC and diverse businesses by 2025.

We’re seeking out and engaging with diverse suppliers, media, and other emerging BIPOC businesses and entrepreneurs.

We’ve committed more than $44 million to expand college prep and career opportunities for BIPOC students

including 16 new scholarships for HBCU (Historically Black Colleges and Universities) students and increased scholarship funding for Teen Tech Center youth.

Best Buy commits up to $10M investment for BIPOC startups

Commitment aims to build a diverse tech ecosystem by breaking down systemic barriers to funding and empowering the next generation of entrepreneurs.
